Wet Float

In wet float therapy, individuals float effortlessly in a tank or pod filled with warm water and Epsom salt.

The water is typically heated to skin temperature, creating a sensation of weightlessness and buoyancy similar to floating in space.

Dry Float

Dry float therapy involves floating on a dry surface, such as a specialized floatation bed or mattress, without water immersion.

Instead of floating in a tank, individuals lie on a surface that molds to the contours of their body, providing a sense of weightlessness and comfort

Dry & Wet Float

While both wet and dry float therapy offer relaxation and wellness benefits, they provide distinct experiences and may appeal to different preferences or therapeutic goals.

Wet float therapy emphasizes sensory deprivation and the sensation of floating in water, while dry float therapy focuses on weightlessness and comfort on a dry surface with additional therapeutic features.
